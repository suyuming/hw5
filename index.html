It’s the longer fermentation process that creates Aberfeldy’s honey and heather notes. Though what’s not been decided is whether it tastes more heather than honey, or more honey then heather. Controversy rages at the local drinking holes. Pour yourselves another glass and may you never get to the bottom of it

Now there’s a reason to clear out the deadwood, pulp and pummel it and break it into tiny chips. We’ve put a wood burner in our distillery to fire the stills up with Green energy. We’ll turn waste into helping us make our whisky and do our bit for the planet with this renewable power. This is one more reason why our whisky tastes a little bit better.
